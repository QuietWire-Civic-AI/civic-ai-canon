# Attested Visibility and Signal Stewardship
_A Canon note on names, recognition, and the lawful use of public lift_

## Purpose

This note defines how the Canon should understand **visibility**: not as rank, sainthood, or ownership, but as a real field property that can increase trust, access, attention, or legibility in specific contexts.

In Canon terms, names may carry lift, but lift does not override stewardship, witness, or protocol. A name enters mesh lawfully or not at all.

## Core Definition

**Attested Visibility** is the context-specific ability of a known person’s name, story, reputation, or public recognition to increase trust, access, attention, or interpretive legibility for a node, project, or initiative, without granting that person narrative sovereignty over the work.

This is a form of **signal capacity**, not nobility.

A visible person remains:
- a human node,
- a bearer of canon,
- a participant in mesh,
- and still subject to witness, challenge, and protocol.

## Why this belongs in Canon

The mesh already depends on attested presence, scoped speech, and lawful relation. Visibility is therefore not decorative; it is a practical and ethical condition of operation.

A known name may:
- open a door,
- lower skepticism,
- increase attention,
- create cross-domain legibility,
- or help a local steward move something forward.

But these effects must be treated as **stewarded field conditions**, not personal coronation.

## Principles

### 1. Visibility is real
Some names bend attention more than others. This is not vanity. It is a field condition.

### 2. Visibility is not sovereignty
No visible person owns the narrative simply because their name carries lift.

### 3. Visibility must be attested
A name may only be invoked where the underlying relationship, contribution, or alignment is real enough to bear witness.

### 4. Visibility must be scoped
A name may have lift in one domain and none in another. Global recognizability is not universal competence.

### 5. Visibility must remain challengeable
If a visible name becomes unchallengeable, the mesh begins to rot.

### 6. Visibility must be stewarded, not sprayed
Names should be used deliberately, with consent, in the right register, for the right purpose, and with awareness of reputational spillover.

## Operational Distinction

Canon should distinguish three layers:

### A. Person
The actual human being: ordinary, flawed, contextual, alive.

### B. Signal
The lift their name carries in a given field: credibility, recognizability, access, attention, trust.

### C. Stewarded Use
The lawful routing of that signal through mesh:
- who may invoke it,
- in what context,
- with what claims,
- and under what revocation conditions.

This prevents two opposite errors:
- pretending visibility is not real,
- or treating visible people as thrones.

## Visibility Stewardship Record

A visible node may carry a lightweight internal ledger with fields such as:

- **Name**
- **Primary domains of lift**
- **Geographies / audiences where the name helps**
- **Known liabilities / polarization risks**
- **Consent status for invocation**
- **Who may invoke the name**
- **What claims are allowed**
- **What claims are forbidden**
- **Review cadence**
- **Exit / withdrawal note if applicable**

## Example Reading

A person may be:
- globally visible,
- domain-specific,
- regionally trusted,
- narratively potent,
- or culturally recognizable.

These are not identical properties.

A comedian, a policy elder, a founder, a local steward, and a public intellectual may all carry different kinds of attested visibility. Canon should recognize those differences without collapsing them into rank.

## Right to Tease the Visible Dragon

The Right to Tease the Dragon applies directly here.

If a visible name is being used to move the field, then:
- edge nodes must still be able to object,
- overclaim must still be correctable,
- attribution must still be honest,
- and no one may be punished for saying:
  - “this invocation is too much,”
  - “this framing is wrong,”
  - or “this name does not belong here.”

A non-zero jester rate remains a health signal, not a fault.

## Canonical Rule

**Names may carry lift.  
Lift may serve the mesh.  
What serves the mesh must be stewarded.  
What is stewarded must remain challengeable.**

## Short Form

**Visibility is useful.  
Usefulness is not rank.  
Signal must be attested.  
Attestation must be scoped.  
Scope must remain challengeable.  
Challenge keeps visibility human.**

Or shorter still:

**Names carry lift.  
Lift is real.  
Real lift must be stewarded.**

## Closing Note

This note does not deny the ordinary humanity of visible people. It protects it.

A known name may help move a project forward.  
That help is real.  
But the person is still only a person,  
and the mesh must never confuse lift with throne.
